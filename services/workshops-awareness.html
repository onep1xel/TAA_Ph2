Disability and Access in Tourism: Opportunities and where to start

Disability is part of diversity – and diversity works for everyone.

If you want to provide great experiences and connect with people, you have to understand who they are. Not only what makes them tick, but what they’re needs might be throughout the period of a visit.

Acting on informed predictions, pre-empting needs and having the confidence to talk about visitor needs is an express lane to happy customers.

By providing insights into of the needs of people with disabilities, we’re able to help illustrate where services and products can excel – delighting customers and vastly improving satisfaction. Moreover, removing bias and gaining a deeper understanding of diversity provides greater satisfaction and broad economic benefits.

Workshops and sessions can cover topics such as the basics of accessible tourism, exceptional CX for a PwD, access and inclusion legislation, marketing and access, media and representation and much more.

We’ve presented and conducted workshops about accessibility to government, non-for-profits and community groups. We’re proud to have worked with Australian Red Cross, Victorian Department of Environment, Land, Water and Planning, presented at the Accessible and Inclusive Tourism Conference and at Victoria’s MPavillion season.
